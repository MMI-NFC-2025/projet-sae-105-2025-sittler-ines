Glossary

Chymistry

Ancient name for chemistry, used until the 17th century. The term encompassed practices that were both emerging science and alchemy.

Alchemy

Ancient discipline, both philosophical and protoscientific, which aimed notably at the transmutation of metals (transforming lead into gold) and the discovery of the panacea, a universal remedy.

Scientific popularization

Action of disseminating scientific knowledge to a non-specialized audience, making it clear, accessible and understandable.

The Matilda Effect

Denial or minimization of the contribution of women scientists to research, whose work is often attributed to their male colleagues. The term was popularized by science historian Margaret W. Rossiter in 1993.

Feminine pedagogy

Teaching approach that takes into account the specificities of the female audience, particularly in historical contexts where women's education was limited.

Scientific 17th century

Period of great intellectual ferment, marked by the Scientific Revolution. It is the century of Galileo, Descartes, Newton, and the birth of the experimental method.
