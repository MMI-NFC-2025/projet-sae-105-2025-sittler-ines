Marie Meurdrac

The Essence of a Woman of Science

Rediscover the first female chemist of the 17th century.

"The Matilda Effect" is the denial or minimization of women's contributions to science. Their work is often attributed to their male colleagues, erasing them from history. This site pays tribute to one of them, Marie Meurdrac, and through her, to all the invisible women of science.

Marie Meurdrac, pioneer of chemistry

In a France where science belongs to men, Marie Meurdrac dares to publish a revolutionary chemistry treatise. Her fight for women's access to knowledge still resonates today.

Charitable and Easy Chemistry in Favor of Ladies

The manual that opened the doors of science to women. First chemistry book written by a woman, Charitable Chemistry democratizes knowledge reserved for the male elite.
